# Russell Wins F1's New Era Opener — But Drivers Aren't Sold

**March 10, 2026**

George Russell won the 2026 Australian GP from pole. Mercedes locked out the podium with Antonelli P2. Leclerc P3 after an intense early battle with Russell. Verstappen finished P6. Piastri crashed before the race even started.

The numbers look great on paper — nearly 3x the overtakes of the 2025 race. But the drivers are split down the middle, and the divide tells you everything about who benefited from the new regs.

**The new rules in 30 seconds:** 50/50 split between combustion and electric power (electric output tripled). Active aero replaces DRS — wings physically change angle on straights vs corners. Cars now run on sustainable fuel from carbon capture. The MGU-H is gone.

**The critics are loud.** Verstappen called it "Formula E on steroids" and "anti-racing." Norris said overtakes are "very artificial" and raised safety concerns — speed differentials of 30-50 kph when one car is harvesting energy while another deploys it. "When someone hits someone at that speed, you're going to fly." Ocon called the cars "painful" to drive. The FIA already had to intervene mid-weekend, cutting recoverable energy in qualifying to stop extreme tactics.

**The winners love it.** Russell: "I personally loved it. The race was really fun to drive." Toto Wolff pushed back on critics, calling drivers "very nostalgic" and arguing fans' opinions matter more than drivers'.

Rule changes could come as early as Round 3 (Japan), with formal discussions planned after China.

**My take:** The pattern is obvious — winners praise the regs, losers criticize them. That doesn't mean the criticism is wrong. Norris's safety concerns about closing speed differentials are legitimate and will only get worse at faster circuits. The real test isn't overtaking stats — it's whether the overtakes feel earned or artificially manufactured by battery management. If F1 tweaks the rules after just two races, it's an admission the regulations needed another year of development.
