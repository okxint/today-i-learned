# Apple just reported $111 billion in revenue for a single quarter. The iPhone is somehow still growing.

$56.99 billion from iPhone. $31 billion from Services (new record). 17% year-over-year growth. In a quarter where everyone expected a slowdown.

---

Apple's fiscal Q2 2026 earnings dropped and the numbers are kind of absurd.

$111.2 billion in revenue. Up 17% year over year. For context, that's more revenue in three months than most Fortune 500 companies generate in a full year. Apple made more money between January and March than Netflix makes in 12 months. Twice.

## The breakdown

**iPhone: $56.99 billion.** Still the engine. Still growing. The iPhone 17 cycle has been stronger than anyone expected, partly driven by Apple Intelligence features that are finally starting to feel useful rather than gimmicky. The camera upgrades and on-device AI processing are doing exactly what Apple hoped - giving people a reason to upgrade even if their old phone works fine.

**Services: $30.9 billion.** New all-time record. Up 16% year over year. This is the number Wall Street cares about most because it's high-margin, recurring, and growing faster than the company average. App Store, Apple Music, iCloud, Apple TV+, Apple Pay, AppleCare. The services business alone is larger than most tech companies.

**Mac: solid.** The M4 chip cycle continued to drive Mac upgrades, especially in the pro and enterprise segments.

**iPad: steady.** Not growing dramatically but not declining either.

**Wearables: flat.** Vision Pro hasn't moved the needle in any meaningful way yet.

## Why this matters

There's a narrative in tech that Apple is a mature company with limited growth potential. That narrative has been wrong for three straight quarters. 17% growth for a company this size is genuinely impressive. Most companies Apple's scale would be thrilled with 5%.

The Services number is the real story. At $31 billion per quarter, Apple's services business is on a $124 billion annual run rate. That's a larger business than Coca-Cola or Nike. And it comes with software margins, not hardware margins.

## The AI question

The Siri revamp with Gemini integration is starting to show up in the numbers, but it's early. The bigger play is Apple Intelligence baked into every new device. Apple doesn't need to win the AI race. They need to make AI useful on their hardware. That's a different, arguably easier problem.

## My take

Apple is doing what Apple always does. They're not first to anything. They weren't first to smartphones, first to smartwatches, first to AI assistants, or first to mixed reality headsets. But they're usually the ones who make it work for normal people.

$111 billion in a quarter. The company that everyone says has peaked just posted its best growth in years. At some point you have to stop predicting Apple's decline and accept that the machine just works.
