# Three points, one game in hand, and the title race Arsenal were running away with is suddenly alive

City beat Arsenal 2-1 at the Etihad. Haaland scored the winner. Arsenal's lead is down to three points with City holding a game in hand. This isn't over.

---

Arsenal were ten points clear in February. Ten. That kind of lead in the Premier League, with this squad, with Arteta's system humming, felt like a procession. People were debating whether this was the best Arsenal team ever, not whether they'd win the league.

And now it's April 20th, the lead is three points, City have a game in hand against Burnley on Wednesday, and if you close your eyes you can hear Pep's press conference voice getting that tone. The calm, measured, "we just focus on the next game" tone that precedes City winning eight in a row.

## What happened at the Etihad

Rayan Cherki opened the scoring in the 15th minute. Good goal, City on the front foot, Etihad loud. Then Donnarumma - City's goalkeeper - gifted Arsenal an equalizer two minutes later. He played a pass straight to Havertz who finished it. 1-1. You could feel the Etihad deflate.

And then nothing happened for 47 minutes. Arsenal were comfortable. They had the momentum after the equalizer. They were doing what Arsenal do - controlling the game without the ball, staying compact, waiting for moments.

Haaland doesn't wait for moments. He creates them. The 64th minute, and he's scored again. 2-1 City. That was the final score.

## The table right now

Arsenal: 70 points, 33 games played.
Man City: 67 points, 32 games played.

If City beat Burnley on Wednesday - which they will - it's 70-70 with City having the momentum, the experience, and arguably the easier remaining fixtures.

## Remaining fixtures

**Arsenal (5 games left):** Newcastle (H), Fulham (H), West Ham (A), Burnley (H), Crystal Palace (A)

**City (6 games left):** Burnley (A), Everton (A), Brentford (H), Bournemouth (H) + two more

Arsenal's schedule looks manageable on paper. Newcastle at home is the toughest one. But Crystal Palace away on the final day could be a nightmare if the title isn't wrapped up by then. Palace at Selhurst Park with nothing to play for is one of the hardest fixtures in the league.

City's schedule is easier. Burnley, Everton, Brentford, Bournemouth. None of those teams are fighting for anything meaningful. This is the kind of run-in City dream about.

## The bigger picture

This is Arsenal's second successive defeat. They lost midweek too. Two losses in a row for a team chasing a title is not just about points dropped - it's about psychology. The dressing room that was relaxed in February is now tense in April. The team that was playing with freedom is now playing with fear.

City have been here before. Multiple times. They've won the league from behind, they've won it on the final day, they've won it when nobody thought they could. Arsenal haven't. This squad hasn't been in a title race this tight this late in the season. 2023-24 they lost it to City at the end. 2024-25 Liverpool ran away with it. Now it's happening again and the pattern is starting to look systemic, not coincidental.

## Liverpool's implosion

Worth noting: Liverpool are completely out of this. They were in the title conversation as recently as March and now they're fighting for a Champions League spot, sitting four points above Chelsea. Their season has collapsed across all fronts - knocked out of the Champions League 4-0 by PSG, FA Cup gone, league title gone. Slot's first full season has gone from "historic treble potential" in November to "at least we got top four" in April.

## My take

I think City win the league. I don't want to type that because every part of me wanted this to be Arsenal's year. But the pattern is too familiar. City get the game in hand, they go level, and then they don't drop points in May. They just don't. They've done this too many times.

Arsenal need to win their remaining five games and hope City slip up at least once. That's the math. And City don't slip up against Burnley, Everton, and Bournemouth. They just don't.

The only scenario where Arsenal win this is if the Champions League semifinal against Atletico takes a physical toll on the squad and they're running on empty for the final three league games. Arteta has to manage minutes carefully. Saka, Saliba, Rice - these players can't play every minute of every game across two competitions.

But if you asked me to bet? City. Again. The seventh title in nine years. Football is cruel sometimes.
