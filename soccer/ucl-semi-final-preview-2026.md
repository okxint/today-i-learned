# Champions League semi-final preview: four teams, two ties, and the most unpredictable bracket in years

PSG vs Bayern on Tuesday. Arsenal vs Atletico on Wednesday. No Real Madrid, no Barcelona, no Liverpool. The old guard is gone. Here's what to expect.

---

The Champions League semi-finals start tomorrow night and for the first time in a very long time, there's no clear favorite. No team that everyone assumes will be in the final. No side with that Champions League aura that makes opponents shrink before kickoff. Real Madrid are gone. Barcelona are gone. Liverpool are gone. What's left is four teams who all believe they can win it and all have genuine reasons to.

## PSG vs Bayern Munich - first leg Tuesday, Parc des Princes

This is the tie everyone wants. The best possession team in Europe against the best attack. PSG haven't been properly tested in these knockouts - they walked through Liverpool 4-0 on aggregate without breaking a sweat. Bayern have been tested - they survived a seven-goal war with Real Madrid - and came out of it looking like a team that knows how to suffer.

PSG's strength is control. They don't give you the ball. They don't give you space. They suffocate you with possession and then hit you on transitions through Dembele who's been the best player in this Champions League by some distance.

Bayern's strength is chaos. They'll trade blows. They'll concede but they'll score more. Kane, Musiala (if fit - he's carrying a knock), Olise, Diaz - this is an attack that scored four against Real Madrid at the Allianz Arena.

The question is simple: can Bayern get enough of the ball to hurt PSG? At the Parc des Princes, against a team that averaged 74% possession against Liverpool, that's going to be hard.

Prediction: PSG 2-1 Bayern. PSG's control is just too good at home. But Bayern will score, and that away goal could be huge for the second leg in Munich.

## Arsenal vs Atletico Madrid - first leg Wednesday, Metropolitano

Nobody wants to watch this game. Everyone will watch it.

Two of the best defensive teams in Europe, managed by two coaches who would rather die than concede. Arteta's Arsenal have the best defense in the Premier League. Simeone's Atletico have been the best defensive team in Europe for the better part of a decade.

Arsenal's problem is they haven't played anyone who defends like Atletico in these knockouts. They beat Sporting 1-0 over two legs. That's great discipline, but Sporting aren't Atletico. Simeone's team just knocked out Barcelona - including two red cards, a VAR controversy, and Lookman scoring the decisive goal on the counter.

Atletico have injury issues at the back. Hancko and Gimenez are both carrying knocks. If either misses out, that changes the dynamic significantly. Arsenal's set pieces are their best weapon, and a weakened Atletico backline defending corners at the Metropolitano is a different proposition to a full-strength one.

Prediction: Atletico 0-0 Arsenal. I know. But this is genuinely how I think this goes. Neither team concedes at the Metropolitano. The tie gets decided at the Emirates.

## The bigger picture

If you're a neutral, pray for PSG vs Arsenal in the final. Dembele vs Saka. Luis Enrique vs Arteta. The team that controls possession vs the team that controls space. That would be a genuinely incredible final.

If you want chaos, hope for Bayern vs Atletico. Seven goals vs zero goals. The best attack vs the best defense. The unstoppable force vs the immovable object. The tactical contrast alone would make it one of the most interesting finals in years.

## My pick

PSG beat Bayern 3-2 on aggregate. Arsenal beat Atletico 1-0 on aggregate (decided at the Emirates, probably Havertz from a corner). PSG vs Arsenal in Budapest on May 30.

But honestly? Any of these four could win the whole thing. That's what makes this bracket special.
