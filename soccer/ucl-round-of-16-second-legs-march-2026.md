# Champions League Round of 16 Second Legs: 8 Ties, 4 Near-Impossible Comebacks, and One Very Nervous Liverpool

**March 17, 2026**

The first legs were chaos. Absolute, beautiful, football-in-its-purest-form chaos. Five of eight ties produced 3+ goals. Three teams are basically already through. And tonight kicks off two days of second legs that will either produce some of the greatest European comebacks ever — or confirm that the knockout rounds have already sorted themselves out.

Here's where every tie stands and what to watch for.

## Tuesday, March 17

### Arsenal vs Bayer Leverkusen (1-1 from first leg)

The most wide-open tie of the round. Arsenal went to the BayArena, got outplayed for 89 minutes, trailed to Robert Andrich's header from a corner — and then Kai Havertz happened. Subbed on, watched Noni Madueke get chopped down in the box, and buried the penalty against his old club with zero hesitation. Two shots on target all game. One of them a pen. Arsenal DNA.

Back at the Emirates, Arteta's side are heavy favorites. They won all eight league phase games — the only team to do that — and their home record in Europe this season is obscene. Leverkusen will need to score, and Arsenal's defense doesn't give those away cheaply.

**The question:** Can Leverkusen actually play their press-and-transition game at the Emirates, or do they sit deep and hope for a counter? Xabi Alonso has to attack. A 0-0 sends them home on away goals under the old rules — wait, those are gone. A 0-0 means extra time. Still, Leverkusen won't want that on Arsenal's turf.

### Manchester City vs Real Madrid (0-3 from first leg)

Valverde. Hat trick. 20th, 27th, 42nd minute. Game over before halftime at the Bernabeu. Three goals, all from midfield runs that City simply couldn't track. Pep's side looked slow, disorganized, and frankly old in patches.

City need four goals without reply at the Etihad. Real Madrid have won their first-leg knockout tie by three or more goals 29 times and qualified in all 28 previous instances. Twenty-eight out of twenty-eight. The math is not on City's side. Neither is the injury list — Gvardiol and Rico Lewis are still out long-term.

But. This is Guardiola vs Ancelotti. Rodri is back. The Etihad under lights in a do-or-die UCL knockout? The atmosphere alone is worth the entrance fee. Mbappe is traveling but might not start (38 goals in 33 games this season, casually). The smart money says Real Madrid cruise into the quarters. The romantic in me says watch the first 20 minutes — if City score early, this gets spicy.

### Chelsea vs PSG (2-5 from first leg)

This one was genuinely heartbreaking for Chelsea. They were level at 2-2 in the 74th minute at the Parc des Princes. Then Vitinha capitalized on a goalkeeper howler, Kvaratskhelia came off the bench and scored twice in the final ten minutes, and suddenly it's 5-2. Three goals conceded in 16 minutes. Peak Chelsea in Europe — give you hope, then rip it away.

Needing to overturn a three-goal deficit against the reigning champions? Chelsea would need to become only the second team in UCL history to pull that off against holders. PSG didn't play at the weekend. They're rested, raring, and ruthless. This one's a formality wearing a competitive disguise.

### Sporting CP vs Bodo/Glimt (0-3 from first leg)

The fairy tale of the round. Bodo/Glimt — from a town of 50,000 people above the Arctic Circle in Norway — went to Lisbon and demolished Sporting 3-0. If you don't know Bodo, they're the club that beat Roma 6-1 in the Conference League back in 2021. They do this. They show up to places they're not supposed to win and tear the script up.

Sporting need a miracle in Lisbon. Bodo need to not completely collapse. One of the easiest second-leg predictions of the round.

## Wednesday, March 18

### Barcelona vs Newcastle (1-1 from first leg)

Lamine Yamal rescued Barcelona with a stoppage-time penalty at St. James' Park. Let that context settle — Barca were heading home with a first-leg defeat against a Newcastle side that hasn't been in the UCL knockout rounds since 2003. Instead, Yamal's composure from the spot means this tie is perfectly level heading to the Camp Nou.

Newcastle away from home in Europe has been a vibe all season, and Eddie Howe's side won't park the bus. An away goal here is gold. Barca have the crowd, the quality, and an 18-year-old who seems allergic to letting his team lose. This could be the match of the entire round.

### Liverpool vs Galatasaray (0-1 from first leg)

The most deceptive scoreline of the first legs. Galatasaray won 1-0 in Istanbul through a Mario Lemina header, and Liverpool were genuinely poor. Not "had an off night" poor — "what is happening" poor. But here's the thing: this is Anfield. Under floodlights. In a European knockout tie. We've seen this movie before. You know how it ends.

One goal and Liverpool are level. Slot's side have the firepower to put three or four past anyone at home. Galatasaray's best chance was taking a bigger lead back to Turkey. A one-goal cushion at Anfield is no cushion at all.

### Bayern Munich vs Atalanta (6-1 from first leg)

Lol. Six-one. In the first leg. Bayern put up a cricket score in Bergamo and Atalanta have to somehow show up at the Allianz Arena and pretend there's something to play for. Kompany's Bayern are terrifying this season. This is a dead rubber disguised as a Champions League fixture. Expect rotations, early subs, and a guard-of-honor energy from the crowd.

### Tottenham vs Atletico Madrid (2-5 from first leg)

Spurs conceded five in Madrid. Five. Against Simeone's Atletico — a team historically associated with 1-0 wins and defensive football turning into a goal machine is the subplot nobody saw coming. Atletico scored five against Tottenham the way old Atletico used to concede zero.

Spurs being Spurs, they'll probably win the second leg 3-0 and give everyone false hope before Atletico score from a corner and end it. Tottenham and hope are a dangerous combination. Ange Postecoglou needs something close to a miracle, and Atletico away from home in a tie they're already controlling is peak "we'll sit deep and counter you to death."

## The Big Picture

Four ties are essentially over (City, Chelsea, Spurs, Atalanta all needing historic comebacks). Two are perfectly poised (Arsenal-Leverkusen, Barca-Newcastle). One is a mild upset waiting to be corrected (Liverpool). And one is Bodo/Glimt continuing to exist in a way that makes no logical sense.

The quarter-final draw is on March 20. If the favorites hold, we're looking at Real Madrid, PSG, Bayern, Atletico, Arsenal, Barcelona, Liverpool, and Bodo/Glimt in the last eight. That last name in that list is absurd and I love it.

**My take:** The tie to watch tonight is Arsenal-Leverkusen. It's the only genuinely 50-50 game on Tuesday. City-Madrid has the narrative, but the scoreline makes it almost academic. Arsenal at home in a tight European tie with quarter-final spots on the line? Arteta's built this squad for exactly these nights. I'm calling Arsenal 2-0, comfortable progression, and Saka doing something filthy to Grimaldo.

Wednesday? Barcelona-Newcastle is appointment viewing. Two teams that both believe they should win, neither with a margin of error. Yamal vs Gordon, Pedri vs Bruno G. If you only watch one game this week, make it that one.

Two nights. Eight ties. Champions League knockout football at its finest.
