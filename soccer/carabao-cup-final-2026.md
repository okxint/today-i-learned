# A 21-Year-Old From Ancoats Just Ended Arsenal's Quadruple Dream at Wembley

**March 23, 2026**

Arsenal came to Wembley chasing history. A quadruple — Premier League, Champions League, FA Cup, and League Cup. No English club has ever done it. They left with nothing but a 2-0 loss and the image of Kepa Arrizabalaga fumbling a cross into the path of a kid who turned 21 yesterday.

Nico O'Reilly. Born in Ancoats, Manchester. Joined City's academy at eight. Scored twice in five minutes to win the Carabao Cup final. Happy birthday to him, I guess.

## What Actually Happened

The first half was exactly what you'd expect from an Arsenal-City cup final — cagey, tactical, neither side willing to commit. Arsenal were without Eze, Timber, and Odegaard. City started Cherki on the wing. Both managers were clearly treating this as a chess match.

Then the 60th minute happened.

Rayan Cherki swung in a cross from the right. Not a particularly dangerous ball. The kind of delivery that any Premier League goalkeeper deals with ten times a game. Except Kepa — starting because both Donnarumma and Raya were unavailable — fumbled it. Parried it straight down. And there was O'Reilly, lurking at the back post, heading into an empty net from three yards.

1-0 City. Arsenal's body language visibly deflated.

Four minutes later, Matheus Nunes played a low cross from the left, and O'Reilly — still running on the dopamine of his first Wembley goal — arrived perfectly to tap it in. 2-0. Game over in the space of 240 seconds.

Arsenal threw everything at it after that. Calafiori hit the post. Gabriel Jesus struck the crossbar. But the scoreline never changed. City's ninth League Cup. Pep's collection grows.

## Who Is Nico O'Reilly?

If you don't follow the academy circuit, you'd be forgiven for not knowing the name before yesterday. But City fans have been watching this kid for years.

Born March 21, 2005 in the Ancoats and Collyhurst neighborhoods of north Manchester. Jamaican heritage through his dad. Joined the City academy at eight. Captained the U18s. Signed a five-year deal in September 2025 after breaking into the first team. Made his England debut in November against Serbia. He plays left-back or midfielder depending on what Guardiola needs — the kind of positional flexibility Pep values above almost everything else.

His 2025-26 season reads: 4 goals, 3 assists. His February brace against Newcastle (2-1 win) signaled he was ready for bigger moments. But nobody expected the biggest moment to come on his 21st birthday in a cup final.

FOX Sports ran a headline after the match: "From Unsung Hero to Lethal Weapon." Outlook India asked if he could be England's MVP at the 2026 World Cup. Twenty-four hours ago, most casual fans couldn't pick him out of a lineup. Football moves fast.

## What This Means for Arsenal

The quadruple is dead. The treble is still alive — Premier League, Champions League, FA Cup. And honestly? This might not be the worst thing for Arteta.

The Carabao Cup has always been the trophy English clubs care about least. Liverpool used to field youth teams in it. Arsenal themselves have historically rotated heavily. Losing it hurts in the moment, but it removes one fixture pile-up from April and May when the league title race, Champions League quarters (Sporting CP), and FA Cup semis will demand every ounce of squad depth.

The bigger concern is Kepa. If he's the backup keeper for the rest of the season, Arsenal's margin for error in the Champions League narrows significantly. One injury to Donnarumma or Raya and you're relying on the man who just fumbled a routine cross in a cup final. That's not a confidence-inspiring safety net.

## What This Means for City

City came into this season as a team "in decline." Lost the league last year. Got knocked out of the Champions League in the round of 16 by Real Madrid again. Questions about whether the post-Haaland era (sold to Real Madrid in 2025) would expose Guardiola's squad.

Nine League Cups now. Nine. More than any other club in the competition's history. And they did it with academy players — O'Reilly, Trafford in goal, a squad that's quietly been rebuilt around younger, hungrier options.

Guardiola's project isn't dying. It's evolving. The names are different but the mentality is the same: show up in finals and win them. City have won their last four domestic cup finals. That's not luck. That's culture.

**My take:** Arsenal losing the Carabao Cup to a 21-year-old academy kid on his birthday is the kind of story football writes when it wants to remind you that form and favorites mean nothing at Wembley. Arsenal are still the better team. They'll probably win the league and might win the Champions League. But for one afternoon, a kid from Ancoats was the best player on the pitch, and that's the magic of the cup.

Kepa, though. Kepa. My brother in Christ, you had one job.
