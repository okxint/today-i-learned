# World Cup 2026: Round 1 Was Chaos, and We Already Know Who's Winning This Thing

*48 teams. 3 host nations. One week in. And we have answers.*

---

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is the biggest sporting event in human history, and it's already delivering. Forty-eight teams. Three countries hosting. Sixteen groups. And after just the first round of matches, we've had a hat-trick from the greatest footballer alive, a 40-year-old goalkeeper from the Portuguese second division becoming more famous than Ronaldo, Germany re-enacting 2014 on Curaçao, and an entire Tuesday where South America went 0-for-4. Zero wins. On the same day. *The continent that invented this sport.*

Let's break it all down - results, stats, storylines - and then I'll tell you exactly who's lifting the trophy on July 19th.

## The Five Stories Defining This World Cup

### 1. Messi Just Broke His Own Record of Being Incomprehensibly Good

Argentina vs Algeria, June 17. Kansas City. 3-0. Hat-trick. **Lionel Messi's 200th cap for Argentina. First man in history to play in six World Cups.**

He scored his 14th, 15th, and 16th World Cup goals. That third one tied Miroslav Klose's all-time record. He got a yellow card for a challenge most outlets called a "clear red" before the first goal. He cried after scoring. Patrick Mahomes posted about it. Rio Ferdinand lost his mind on TV.

Honestly? What more is there to say. The man is 38 years old at a World Cup. Defending champion. Hat-trick in his first game. If this is the last dance - and it might genuinely be - he's making sure we remember every step.

---

### 2. Vozinha: The Goalkeeper That Broke the Internet (and Spain)

June 15. Atlanta. Spain - ranked #2 in the world, European champions, pre-tournament favorites at +500 - played Cape Verde. A country of 600,000 people. Ranked #67. World Cup debutants.

**Spain had 27 shots. 734 passes. 74% possession. The most shots by a team that failed to score at a World Cup since 1966.**

Final score: 0-0.

The man responsible: **Vozinha**, a 40-year-old goalkeeper who plays in Portugal's *second division*. Seven saves. Made Ferran Torres, Pedri, and company look like they were shooting at a brick wall. He went from 500,000 Instagram followers to **8 million overnight.** His mother was initially denied a visa to watch the game and then granted a special exemption after the whole thing went viral.

Vozinha is currently the most famous person in soccer. Ronaldo who?

---

### 3. Germany Did Germany Things (Again)

Germany vs Curaçao: **7-1**.

Nmecha. Schlotterbeck. Havertz (pen). Musiala. Brown. Undav. Havertz again. Curaçao - the smallest nation ever to qualify for a World Cup - pulled one back through Comenecia in the 21st minute. Bold. Unfortunate. Completely irrelevant to the outcome.

Germany's xG was 4.22. They scored 7. Pure, slightly terrifying efficiency.

The coaching matchup was accidentally funny: 78-year-old Dick Advocaat managing Curaçao against 38-year-old Julian Nagelsmann - the largest age gap between coaches in World Cup history. Advocaat has been in football longer than some of the German players' parents have been alive.

Everyone watching this game spent exactly 3 minutes thinking about the Brazil 7-1 from 2014. I'm not saying Nagelsmann planned it. I'm just saying he absolutely planned it.

---

### 4. Mbappe Rewrites French History, But France Was Almost Embarrassing in the First Half

France vs Senegal, June 16. New York/New Jersey. 3-1.

**Kylian Mbappe scored twice to become France's all-time leading scorer at 58 goals**, passing Giroud's 57. Then added a stoppage-time thunderbolt to seal it.

Here's the part nobody's talking about enough: France's xG in the first half was **0.02**. *Zero point zero two.* Senegal had 0.47. Senegal had France genuinely rattled for 45 minutes. Missed chances. Near misses. Absolute chaos.

France flipped a switch in the second half, scored three, and is now the tournament favorite at +410. But that first half is a warning. Teams that get in front of France early - especially in the knockouts - are going to cause problems.

---

### 5. Haaland Finally Gets His World Cup Moment

Norway failed to qualify in 2018. Failed in 2022. Erling Haaland - the most prolific striker in Premier League history - had never played a World Cup minute.

June 16. Boston. Norway vs Iraq. 4-1. **Haaland scored twice.** His second goal came when the Iraqi goalkeeper kicked the ball directly into him and it rebounded into the net. That was his 2nd World Cup goal. It was also very much a Haaland goal.

Norway is in a group with France. That potential knockout collision would be the most watched match in Scandinavian TV history.

## Group Standings - The Quick Version

Groups worth watching going into matchday 2:

- **Group H** (Spain, Cape Verde, Saudi Arabia, Uruguay) - all four on 1 point each. Spain needs to sort themselves out fast.
- **Group C** (Scotland, Brazil, Morocco, Haiti) - *Scotland* is leading this group. Brazil drew. This is not a drill.
- **Group I** (France, Norway, Senegal, Iraq) - France and Norway both on 3 points. The Group of Death quietly snuck up on us.
- **Group J** (Argentina + Algeria + others) - Messi's Argentina top, but everyone in this group now has a story to tell.

## Who Wins the 2026 World Cup

Let me stop hedging and just say it.

**France lifts the trophy on July 19th.**

Here's why:

**They have the deepest squad at the tournament.** Mbappe as a striker-nine hybrid, Barcola off the bench, Camavinga in midfield - France can rotate without dropping quality. Their xG numbers over the last 18 months of Nations League and qualifying were consistently top 2 globally. Even in a sloppy first half against Senegal, the talent eventually took over.

**Mbappe at 26 is unstoppable when he wants to be.** He's now France's all-time leading scorer. He has something to prove after a complicated Qatar 2022 final where he scored a hat-trick and still lost. He looked sharp in spurts vs Senegal. That will only get better.

**France peaks in knockout rounds.** Their 3-1 result against Senegal came despite a terrible first 45 minutes. If they come out firing from the start in the rounds that matter, teams are done before halftime.

**Deschamps knows how to manage a tournament.** Not just individual games - a whole tournament. France grinds when they need to and explodes when the moment calls for it. That's worth more than anything over 7 games.

---

## The Three Teams That Could Stop Them

**Argentina** is the biggest threat. Messi with 3 goals in game one, 200th cap, defending champion, squad built around winning right now. They go deep or go home and Messi knows it. If this is his last World Cup, he's not going quietly.

**Germany** is dangerous in a way that's hard to explain until they're already in your final. 7-1 in the opener, full squad available, Musiala and Havertz combining in ways that cause problems for everyone. If the bracket falls right for them, they can win this.

**England** is technically in the conversation but the 4-2 vs Croatia - Croatia equalizing twice before England pulled away - showed defensive vulnerability that better teams will exploit. Kane, Bellingham, Rashford are all capable of winning a game by themselves. England's defensive shape wasn't convincing though. They have the talent to win it. They also have the history of not winning it. Pick your poison.

---

## The Teams That Are Out of Their Depth

**Portugal** - Ronaldo played 90 minutes with **zero shots on target** against DR Congo. João Neves scored in the 5th minute and then Portugal proceeded to look like they'd never played football before. Ronaldo is 41. The entire national team project built around a 41-year-old who refuses to acknowledge he is 41 is catching up with them.

**Spain** - Most talented squad in the tournament on paper. But the Cape Verde 0-0 isn't a blip. It's a pattern. Spain can be nullified by compact defending and every knockout round opponent will now study that Cape Verde tape obsessively.

**Brazil** - Missing Neymar (calf), Rodrygo (ACL), and Militão (hamstring). Drew 1-1 with Morocco. Vinícius scored but Alisson needed a double save late to preserve even a point. Endrick is the future. Right now in 2026, they might not have enough.

## My Take

This tournament is delivering what the 48-team format promised - chaos, upsets, and storylines from places that have never had them before. A 40-year-old second-division keeper held Spain scoreless in their debut. Scotland leads a group containing Brazil. Messi scored a hat-trick on his 200th cap on a Wednesday night in Kansas City.

And we're one round in.

My prediction is France. But if Messi gets to a final, the right team wins regardless of who's across from him. There's a version of this World Cup where Messi equals or breaks the all-time scoring record, lifts the trophy again, and retires on the spot. And if that happens, it's the greatest individual career in sport - finished exactly the way it should be.

Very on brand.
