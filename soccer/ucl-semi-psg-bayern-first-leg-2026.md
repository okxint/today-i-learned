# PSG 5-4 Bayern Munich: nine goals, two braces, and a semi-final that broke every rule

Dembele and Kvaratskhelia scored twice each. Bayern scored four away goals. PSG lead by one going to Munich. The second leg might actually break football.

---

Nine goals in a Champions League semi-final first leg. Nine. That number alone tells you everything and nothing at the same time.

PSG vs Bayern at the Parc des Princes was supposed to be a chess match between two of the best-coached teams in Europe. Luis Enrique's possession game against Kompany's press. Control against chaos. What we got instead was both teams deciding that defending was optional and attack was mandatory.

## How do you even summarize this

Kane opened the scoring in the 17th minute. 0-1 Bayern. Cool, normal Champions League stuff.

Then the game lost its mind.

Kvaratskhelia equalized in the 24th. Joao Neves made it 2-1 in the 33rd. Olise - who's been electric all tournament - made it 3-1 in the 41st. Then Dembele scored right before halftime. 4-1 PSG heading into the break.

At halftime, the game looked done. PSG were cruising. Bayern looked broken. The aggregate was effectively over after 45 minutes.

The second half said otherwise.

Kvaratskhelia got his second in the 56th. 5-1. Now it really is over, right?

Dembele made it 5-2 in the 58th with his second. Wait - no. Let me re-read the scorers. The goal sequence in the second half went: Kvaratskhelia (56'), Dembele (58'), Upamecano (65'), Diaz (68'). PSG scored twice, then Bayern scored twice in three minutes. 5-2 became 5-4.

Bayern scored three goals in the second half. At the Parc des Princes. In a Champions League semi-final. Down 4-1 at halftime.

## The numbers

- 9 goals total
- 30+ shots combined
- Bayern scored 4 away goals
- PSG lead the aggregate by just 1
- Bayern need ONE goal in Munich to level it

For context: the Bayern vs Madrid quarter-final was 7 goals and people called it a classic. This was 9. In a semi-final.

## What this means for the second leg

PSG lead 5-4. That sounds comfortable until you remember that Bayern just scored three times at the Parc des Princes in 13 minutes. One goal at the Allianz Arena and the aggregate is level.

Bayern have already shown this season that the Allianz Arena is a fortress. They beat Real Madrid 4-3 there. They don't fold at home. And now they go into the second leg knowing they only need one goal to reset everything.

PSG's problem is psychological. They were 5-1 up and nearly threw it away. That kind of collapse - even if you technically win - leaves marks. The dressing room that was celebrating at halftime was holding on for dear life by the 70th minute.

## My take

I still think PSG go through, but my confidence has dropped from 80% to about 55%. The away goals don't count double anymore, so Bayern genuinely just need one goal to level it and then it's effectively a one-off game.

The second leg is May 6 at the Allianz Arena. If this first leg produced 9 goals, I genuinely have no idea what the second leg will look like. Double digits isn't a joke. It might actually happen.

This Champions League campaign has now produced: a seven-goal classic in the quarter-finals (Bayern 4-3 Madrid) and a nine-goal semi-final. If the second leg somehow tops this, we're watching the greatest Champions League knockout stage in the history of the competition.
