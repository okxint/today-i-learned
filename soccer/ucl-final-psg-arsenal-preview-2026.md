# PSG vs Arsenal in Budapest: the Champions League final nobody expected and everyone wanted

Arsenal's first final since 2006. PSG's first-ever. Thirty-one goals across four semi-final legs. The most anticipated final in a generation.

**Date:** 2026-05-12
**Topic:** Soccer / Champions League

---

After four semi-final legs that collectively produced more goals than some entire tournaments, Europe has its two finalists. PSG vs Arsenal. The club that invented modern football spending vs the club that's been waiting twenty years for this moment. Budapest, May 31.

I've watched a lot of Champions League. This is the final people have been wanting to see for two seasons.

## How we got here

Arsenal needed a second leg at the Emirates to finish Atletico. After the 1-1 draw in Madrid — two penalties, zero open play goals, pure Simeone — the second leg was exactly what you'd expect from Arsenal at home with everything on the line. The Emirates was a wall of noise. Arteta set up to press high and never let Atletico breathe.

Odegaard scored in the first half. One goal, that's all it took. Arsenal held the lead, Atletico couldn't break them down at the other end, and when the final whistle went the Emirates erupted. First Champions League final since 2006. Twenty years of hurt, done.

PSG vs Bayern was somehow even more absurd than the first leg. After the 5-4 first leg chaos at the Parc des Princes, Bayern came to Munich needing one goal. They got it in the 21st minute. Aggregate level. Then the game turned into exactly the kind of battle you'd expect — tight, tense, both managers actually coaching for the first time in the tie. PSG scored in the 78th. 1-1 on the night, 6-5 on aggregate. PSG through. The Allianz went quiet.

## The matchup

PSG have been building toward this for fifteen years. They've won Ligue 1 so many times it stopped being a story. They spent billions to get to this stage and kept falling short — losing to Real Madrid, to Bayern, to teams that matched their talent with experience and hunger. This squad is different. Dembele and Kvaratskhelia on the wings is genuinely one of the best wide pairs in Europe. Joao Neves has solved the midfield problem that plagued them for years. And they've now beaten Inter (quarters), Bayern (semis), Real Madrid (group stage). This isn't a fluke run.

Arsenal have never won the Champions League. That matters more than it sounds, because the players and manager know it. But this team doesn't carry that baggage in a way that makes them nervous — they carry it in a way that makes them hungry. Gyokeres has been one of the best strikers in Europe this season. Odegaard is the best playmaker not named anyone at Real Madrid. And Arteta's system — the high press, the coordinated movements, the absolute defensive discipline — is now one of the best-drilled in the world.

## Why this is hard to call

PSG have the better squad on paper. More creative players, more Champions League experience at the squad level, more tactical options. They beat Bayern away from home in the second leg when the pressure was maximum. That's not a nothing result.

Arsenal have the better structure and the better season domestically. Winning the Premier League — the hardest league in the world — with the kind of consistency they showed is not a minor achievement. They're not in a Budapest hotel running on fumes. They peaked at the right time.

The thing that makes this genuinely coin-flip: PSG have match-winners capable of doing something unrepeatable in one moment. Dembele's pace, Kvaratskhelia's dribbling, Dembele off the ball — any one of them can win a game by themselves in a way Arsenal's attackers, brilliant as they are, don't quite operate in. Arsenal win through structure. PSG can win through individual brilliance. Both are valid Champions League strategies. One final decides which philosophy is right.

## The history angle

PSG's largest shareholder has been trying to buy a Champions League trophy for fifteen years. That framing gets boring, but the sporting reality behind it is real: this is a club that has never won it, that has assembled squads that should have won it multiple times, and that has fallen at the final hurdle or before it every time.

Arsenal last won a major European trophy in 1994. Their 2006 final against Barcelona — the one everyone references — was remarkable for different reasons: Lehmann's red card in the 18th minute, Sol Campbell's header for the lead, Eto'o and Belletti breaking their hearts. This squad has no players from that night. They don't need to exorcise ghosts. They just want to win.

## My take

Arsenal 2-1.

I've convinced myself of this through logic and I'm aware that's exactly the kind of confidence that gets humbled in Champions League finals. But here's the reasoning: PSG's wide players are devastating, but Arsenal's fullbacks — specifically how they defend wide — have been exceptional all season. Saka and White on the right, Timber and Calafiori on the left, have dealt with every wide threat thrown at them. Dembele and Kvaratskhelia are the best test of that yet.

Arsenal will not try to play an open game. They'll press high for 20 minutes to establish dominance, then transition into a mid-block that makes PSG go sideways. One moment of quality from Gyokeres or Odegaard. PSG equalize from a moment of individual brilliance, because that's what they do. Then Arsenal nick it.

That's the film in my head. Real football never looks like the film in your head.

Budapest on May 31. Set an alarm.
