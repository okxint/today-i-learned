# Atletico 1-1 Arsenal: both goals from penalties, zero from open play, and a semi-final that was exactly what everyone expected

Simeone vs Arteta. Two defenses. Two penalties. Zero goals from open play. The tie goes to the Emirates with everything still to play for.

---

Twenty-four hours after PSG and Bayern produced nine goals and one of the greatest Champions League games ever played, Atletico and Arsenal walked onto the same stage and chose violence of a completely different kind.

Both goals were penalties. Neither team scored from open play. The xG was barely above 1 combined. And somehow, despite all of that, it was completely gripping.

## The game

Gyokeres won and converted a penalty in the first half to put Arsenal ahead. Hancko fouled him in the box - clumsy, unnecessary, the kind of mistake Simeone's teams almost never make. Gyokeres stepped up and buried it. 1-0 Arsenal at the Metropolitano.

That should have been the story. Arsenal leading away from home in a Champions League semi-final against the most defensively disciplined team in Europe. Arteta's game plan working to perfection.

Then Atletico got a penalty of their own in the second half. 1-1. Both goals from the spot. Neither defense broken from open play.

## The contrast

This has to be said: PSG 5-4 Bayern and Atletico 1-1 Arsenal happened in the same competition, in the same round, 24 hours apart. One produced nine goals from open play. The other produced zero. One was a basketball game disguised as football. The other was chess with shin pads.

Both were completely compelling for entirely different reasons. That's what makes the Champions League the best competition in sport.

## What this means

Arsenal take a 1-1 draw back to the Emirates. That's a good result. Not a great one - they had the lead and couldn't hold it - but going home with an away goal and a level tie is exactly what Arteta would have planned for.

The second leg at the Emirates on May 5 is now effectively a one-off game. Arsenal at home, needing to win or draw to go through (assuming they score). Their home record this season is elite. The Emirates under the lights for a Champions League semi-final second leg will be a different animal.

## My take

Arsenal go through. The Emirates will be worth a goal by itself. Simeone will set up to defend and counter, which is exactly what Arteta knows how to deal with. One set piece, one moment of quality, and Arsenal are in their first Champions League final since 2006.

PSG vs Arsenal in Budapest. That's still my prediction. But this Atletico side won't make it easy.
