# Arsenal are 6 points clear and the title race they were losing two weeks ago might already be over

Gyokeres scored twice against Fulham. Arsenal won 3-0. City have two games in hand but now need to win all five remaining matches just to have a chance. The pressure has completely flipped.

---

Two weeks ago I wrote that City would win the league. Arsenal had just lost at the Etihad, the gap was down to three points with City holding a game in hand, and the pattern was too familiar. City close the gap, City win in May, City lift the trophy. We've seen this movie before.

Arsenal just rewrote the script.

## What happened

Arsenal beat Fulham 3-0 at home. Gyokeres scored twice in the first half, Saka added a third. Professional, clinical, the kind of performance that title-winning teams produce when they need to respond.

The gap is now 6 points. City have two games in hand, so if they win both, it's back to level on games with City still 6 behind. City would then need Arsenal to drop points in their remaining three games while winning all of theirs.

## The math

Arsenal: 76 points, 35 played, 3 remaining (West Ham A, Burnley H, Crystal Palace A)
City: 70 points, 33 played, 5 remaining (Everton A, Brentford H, Crystal Palace H, Bournemouth A, Aston Villa H)

If Arsenal win their remaining three games: 85 points.
City's maximum: 85 points (win all five).

So even in the best-case scenario for City, it comes down to goal difference. Arsenal's is currently +41 vs City's +37. That's a meaningful gap.

For City to win the title, they need to win all five AND hope Arsenal drop points somewhere. West Ham away and Crystal Palace away are the danger games for Arsenal. But Burnley at home should be a banker.

## The Gyokeres factor

Viktor Gyokeres is the signing of the season and it's not close. He's scored in the Champions League semi-final and now twice in a must-win league game. The man Arsenal bought to be their missing striker has been exactly that. The goals that Arteta's team couldn't find last season are flowing this time.

## My take

I'm reversing my prediction from two weeks ago. Arsenal win the league. The momentum has shifted completely. Beating Fulham 3-0 after the Atletico draw and the City loss shows a team that knows how to respond under pressure.

City still have to play five games in 20 days while Arsenal play three. That schedule compression matters. If City drop even one game, it's done.

The title race isn't officially over. But it feels like it might be.
