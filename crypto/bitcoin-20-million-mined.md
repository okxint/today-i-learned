# Bitcoin Just Mined Its 20 Millionth Coin. Only 1 Million Left. The Next One Takes 114 Years.

**March 15, 2026**

On March 9, at block height 939,999, the Foundry USA mining pool collected a 3.125 BTC reward and quietly pushed Bitcoin past a milestone that will never happen again. Twenty million coins now exist. One million remain. And that last million? It won't finish minting until roughly 2140 - long after everyone reading this is dead.

Let that math sink in for a second. It took 17 years to mine 95% of all Bitcoin that will ever exist. The remaining 5% will take 114 years. If Bitcoin were an hourglass, almost all the sand is already at the bottom.

## Why This Number Matters More Than You Think

The raw number is cool but not the real story. The real story is what's happening around the number.

**The lost coins problem.** Between 2.3 million and 3.7 million BTC are permanently gone - forgotten passwords, dead hardware wallets, early miners who moved on or passed away without sharing keys. Satoshi's own ~1.1 million BTC haven't moved since 2009. So when people say "20 million coins exist," the honest number is closer to 16-17 million in actual circulation. That's it. For an entire planet of 8 billion people.

**The halving squeeze.** Miners currently earn 3.125 BTC per block - about 450 BTC per day, ~164,000 per year. The next halving in 2028 cuts that to 1.5625 BTC. Then 0.78125. Then 0.390625. Each halving doesn't just reduce supply - it raises the existential question for miners: is the block reward alone enough to keep the lights on? The hardware arms race has gone from 98 J/TH to sub-15 J/TH in eight years, a 7x efficiency jump. Miners running anything above 25 J/TH are already operating at a loss in most electricity markets. The post-halving shakeout is real, and it's getting bloodier each cycle.

## The Week Everything Else Lined Up

The 20 million milestone didn't happen in a vacuum. It dropped during possibly the most consequential week for crypto regulation in U.S. history.

**The SEC-CFTC Peace Treaty.** On March 11 - two days after the milestone - the SEC and CFTC signed a Memorandum of Understanding that effectively ends their decade-long turf war over crypto. The deal, born out of "Project Crypto" (a joint task force launched in January), draws a clear line: the CFTC gets primary spot-market authority over decentralized "digital commodities" like Bitcoin and Ethereum. The SEC keeps securities-like tokens and primary offerings. They'll share data, coordinate enforcement, hold joint meetings, and - critically - confer on charges before filing them instead of tripping over each other in court.

SEC Chairman Paul Atkins and CFTC Chairman Michael Selig signed off on it. The crypto industry exhaled so hard you could feel it from across the ocean. Analysts are saying this could unlock trillions in institutional capital that's been sitting on the sideline waiting for exactly this kind of clarity.

**Saylor keeps buying.** On the same day Bitcoin hit 20 million coins, Michael Saylor's Strategy announced another purchase - 17,994 BTC for $1.28 billion at an average of $70,946 per coin. Total holdings: 738,731 BTC. The man now controls roughly 3.7% of all Bitcoin in circulation. Public companies collectively hold over 1 million BTC. Let that one breathe for a minute.

**BlackRock's staked ETH ETF.** BlackRock launched the iShares Staked Ethereum Trust ETF on Nasdaq in early March. A product type that literally didn't exist in the US a year ago. Meanwhile, Kraken dropped an open-source CLI giving AI agents direct access to crypto markets, and Nasdaq announced a partnership with Kraken to offer tokenized stocks. The infrastructure is being built in real-time.

## Bitcoin at $69K - The Disconnect

Here's the weird part. All of this - the scarcity milestone, the regulatory clarity, the institutional buying - and Bitcoin is trading at roughly $69,000. Down 46% from its October peak. Still below what most people would expect given the fundamentals.

Three reasons for the gap:

**1. Macro is king.** Liquidity and interest rate expectations still dominate short-term price action. Crypto doesn't trade in a vacuum, no matter how good the narrative is. Raphael Zagury, CEO of Elektron Energy, put it well: the milestone won't drive short-term price, but the underlying scarcity principle is what matters long-term.

**2. The retail-to-institutional handoff.** Saylor himself predicted this - 2026 is the year the baton passes from retail traders to bankers. Charles Schwab and Citibank are planning custody and credit services by mid-2026. Grayscale's 2026 outlook is literally titled "Dawn of the Institutional Era." When the big money finally deploys, it won't move the price like retail FOMO does. It'll move it slowly, then all at once.

**3. The efficient supply illusion.** People see "20 million coins" and think abundance. The reality is closer to 16 million liquid coins for the entire planet, with new supply being cut in half every four years, and the largest holders actively removing coins from circulation. The math doesn't add up to $69K forever.

## The Fidelity Statement Nobody Talked About

Fidelity Investments - one of the largest asset managers on Earth - released a statement on March 9 acknowledging the milestone and suggesting it "could enhance Bitcoin's appeal as a scarce asset and potentially influence its allocation in diversified portfolios." They highlighted diminishing supply as "a hedge against inflationary pressures in traditional currencies."

This is Fidelity. Not Crypto Twitter. Not a Bitcoin maxi podcast. A $5 trillion asset manager casually suggesting that Bitcoin's scarcity makes it a legitimate portfolio allocation. Two years ago, that sentence would've been unthinkable from a firm at this scale. Now it's a press release.

## The Next 114 Years

Kraken Chief Economist Thomas Perfumo nailed it: "This is the first monetary system in history with a fully predictable policy written in code." Actually, that was Raphael Zagury - but the point stands regardless of who said it. No central bank, no committee, no emergency rate cuts. The supply curve was decided in 2009 and it hasn't changed once.

The next halving is in 2028. By then, over 96.8% of all Bitcoin will have been issued. The one after that pushes it past 98%. Eventually, miners will rely entirely on transaction fees rather than block rewards - the economics of the entire network shifts from inflation-funded to fee-funded security.

## My Take

I've been covering crypto since the DeFi Summer days and this is the first milestone that feels genuinely different. Not because of the number - 20 million is just a number. Because of the timing.

You have a once-in-history supply milestone landing in the same week as the most important U.S. crypto regulatory breakthrough ever, while the largest asset managers on Earth are building infrastructure to onboard institutional capital, while public companies are accumulating at a pace that would make early miners jealous.

And the price is $69K.

I'm not a financial advisor and this isn't investment advice. But I will say this: every time Bitcoin's fundamentals have dramatically outpaced its price, the correction has eventually come. Not on anyone's timeline. Not on any analyst's prediction. But it comes.

The difference this time is that when it comes, the supply faucet is barely dripping. And in 2028, it gets cut in half again.

Twenty million down. One million to go. The clock is ticking - and it's ticking very, very slowly.
