# The Trial That Could Break Up Live Music's Monopoly — Then Didn't

**March 10, 2026**

The DOJ's antitrust trial against Live Nation/Ticketmaster opened March 3 with a bombshell week of testimony — then settled on March 9, just six days in. The settlement stunned everyone, including the judge.

**The monopoly by numbers:** Ticketmaster controls ~80% of primary ticketing at major venues. Live Nation holds 60-65% of concert promotion, manages 400+ artists, and owns 265+ venues including 60 of the top 100 amphitheaters. 75% of ticket sales flow through venues locked into exclusive Ticketmaster contracts.

**The damning evidence:** The DOJ played a recorded call of CEO Michael Rapino threatening to withhold concerts from Brooklyn's Barclays Center if they switched from Ticketmaster to SeatGeek. "Tough to deliver concerts," he said, suggesting he'd route acts to the rival UBS Arena instead.

**The settlement (March 9):** No breakup. $280-300M in damages. Divest up to 13 amphitheaters. Cap service fees at 15%. Limit exclusivity contracts to 4 years. Open 50% of tickets at owned venues to competing platforms.

**The backlash:** 26 states plus DC rejected the deal and will continue the lawsuit. New York's AG said it "fails to address the monopoly at the center of this case." NIVA called it "a failure of the justice system" with no protections for fans, artists, or independent venues. The judge publicly scolded both sides for secretly negotiating while the trial was underway. Live Nation's stock surged — investors relieved a full breakup was avoided.

**My take:** This settlement is a masterclass in corporate damage control. Live Nation gave up just enough to claim reform while keeping the monopoly intact. 13 amphitheaters out of 265? A 15% fee cap that probably matches what they'd charge anyway? The 26 states continuing the fight are right — this doesn't change the structural problem. The real story is that the DOJ blinked after one week of trial. If the recorded Rapino call wasn't enough to push for a breakup, nothing will be.
