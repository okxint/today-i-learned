# Anthropic just became the most valuable AI startup on earth. $900 billion. Let that sit.

Two months ago they were worth $380 billion. This week they're closing a $30 billion round at $900 billion. That's a 2.4x jump in under four months. OpenAI is now the second most valuable AI company. And somehow the most interesting part of this story isn't the money.

---

Anthropic is closing a $30 billion funding round at a $900 billion+ valuation. It should close the week of May 26. For context, OpenAI was valued at $852 billion in March. So the "safe AI" company that started as a splinter from OpenAI — the one that used to be the underdog — just leapfrogged it on valuation.

## The numbers don't make sense until you look at the revenue

The skeptic's reaction is: $900 billion for a company that doesn't have a public product as dominant as ChatGPT? How?

Here's the answer. Anthropic's quarterly revenue doubled. Their Q2 2026 projection is $10.9 billion annualized. And crucially — they turned their first-ever quarterly operating profit. That last detail is why the number is $900B and not $500B. A profitable AI company at hypergrowth isn't a science experiment anymore. It's a business.

The revenue flywheel is Claude-as-infrastructure. Not Claude.ai the consumer app, but the Claude API powering everyone else's products. Enterprise contracts. AWS Bedrock integration. The operator model where companies build on top of Claude rather than building their own models. That bet — be the picks-and-shovels company, not the gold rush chaser — is paying off.

## The OpenAI-Anthropic race just went public

The same week Anthropic's round leaked, OpenAI filed a confidential S-1 targeting a $1 trillion IPO in September. So you have the two most important AI companies in the world in a full sprint — one trying to IPO at $1T, one closing private rounds that keep pace.

These two companies are run completely differently. OpenAI is pushing hard to remove its nonprofit structure and become a fully for-profit entity. Anthropic is a Public Benefit Corporation with Constitutional AI baked into its charter. Different cultures, different risk profiles, different investor dynamics. But they're running the same race: whoever controls the model layer controls the stack.

Both have massive backing from the hyperscalers — Microsoft locked in on OpenAI, Google and Amazon split across Anthropic. The AI infrastructure war is really a war between Microsoft and the Google/Amazon coalition, with the model companies as proxies.

## Why it jumped from $380B to $900B in four months

The short answer: revenue acceleration plus operating profit plus the market deciding that AI isn't a bubble anymore.

The longer answer is about the "safety premium." Anthropic has always positioned itself as the responsible choice — the one that enterprise legal and compliance teams feel good about. That positioning used to feel like a disadvantage (who buys the slower, more careful model?). Now it looks like a moat. Regulated industries, healthcare, finance, government — they're all coming to Anthropic specifically because of the Constitutional AI framing. The TAM for "AI I'm legally allowed to deploy" is massive, and Anthropic owns it.

Also: Google I/O dropped Gemini 3.5 Flash and Gemini Spark the same week. The AI calendar is so packed right now that a competing announcement from Google barely registered.

## My take

The $900B number matters more than OpenAI's $1T IPO target. Here's why.

OpenAI going public at $1T is a financial event. It prices in future expectations, depends on market conditions, and gives investors an exit. It might be worth $1T in 2026 and $400B in 2028 if the market turns or a better model shows up. Public valuations move.

Anthropic getting to $900B in private markets means sophisticated institutional investors — people who don't have to buy in — decided that number was fair at this specific moment. There's no liquidity pressure, no IPO narrative to hype, no retail FOMO. Just funds writing enormous checks because the revenue numbers justify it.

The real story is what this means for everyone building on these platforms. At $900B private, Anthropic isn't going anywhere. The API isn't getting shut down. The operator model is stable. For PMs and engineers who made the bet that Claude was the right infrastructure layer to build on — this week was confirmation.

One thing worth watching: revenue per token is still falling across every model provider. The unit economics of AI inference keep getting better for customers, which is great for adoption but eventually creates pressure on margins. The question isn't whether Anthropic is worth $900B today — clearly investors think so. The question is whether that revenue growth rate holds when the next generation of models commoditizes what Claude 4 does today.

They have 90 days before the next benchmark drop. Then we'll know.
