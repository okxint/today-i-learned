# The AI demand bubble nobody's talking about, and why Anthropic might be the only one who gets it

Anthropic ditched flat-rate enterprise pricing. CNBC says AI demand metrics are broken. OpenAI admits unlimited plans "don't make sense." Here's what's actually happening.

---

There's a CNBC piece from last week that should be getting way more attention than it is. The headline: "AI demand is inflated, and only Anthropic is being realistic."

The core argument is simple. The main demand signal for AI - token consumption - looks explosive on paper. But a huge chunk of that usage is coming from agentic workflows and power users who are consuming millions of tokens per session under flat-rate pricing plans. The demand is real, but the revenue model behind it is broken.

## What Anthropic did

Anthropic moved away from flat-rate enterprise pricing and switched to per-token billing. No more bundled token allowances in seat deals. Every token costs money, and the price reflects actual usage.

Their rates on the latest models: $5 per million input tokens, $25 per million output tokens. Simple. You use more, you pay more. You use less, you pay less.

## Why flat-rate pricing broke

Agentic AI is the answer. When Claude Code launched with a $200/month Max plan, heavy users were running workflows that would've cost $3,000-5,000 at per-token rates. The math just didn't work. One power user could blow through what Anthropic expected an entire team to use.

OpenAI's Nick Turley said it out loud: "Having an unlimited plan is like having an unlimited electricity plan. It just doesn't make sense."

He's right. And it's weird that it took this long for someone senior at OpenAI to acknowledge it publicly.

## The demand illusion

Here's the part that matters for anyone building on or investing in AI.

When a company reports "500% growth in token consumption," that number is real. People are using the tools. But if 40% of that consumption is coming from heavy agentic users on flat-rate plans, the revenue growth doesn't match the usage growth. The demand looks explosive, but the economics underneath it are soft.

This is the bubble nobody's talking about. Not a bubble in AI capability - the models are genuinely getting better. A bubble in AI demand metrics. Companies are reporting usage numbers that look incredible, but the revenue per token is declining because of how the pricing is structured.

Anthropic's move to per-token billing is essentially them saying: we'd rather have honest numbers. If usage drops when people start paying per token, that tells us something important about how much of the "demand" was real vs how much was just people running expensive workflows because it was free.

## The numbers right now

OpenAI: $25 billion annualized revenue. Reportedly exploring an IPO for late 2026.

Anthropic: approaching $19 billion annualized revenue. Enterprise clients apparently describe Claude as having "become a religion." At a recent industry conference, vendors were explicitly saying they preferred Claude over ChatGPT - which is new. A year ago that conversation didn't exist.

## What this means for the market

If Anthropic's right, and per-token billing becomes the industry standard, two things happen:

1. Reported demand numbers across the industry come down. Not because people stop using AI, but because the inflated usage from flat-rate plans disappears. This will look like a "demand correction" even though it's just better measurement.

2. Companies that built their AI strategy around cheap unlimited tokens will have to rethink. If your AI agent costs $0.50 per run on flat-rate and $4.00 per run on per-token pricing, every product manager in the company suddenly cares about token efficiency.

## My take

Anthropic is making the right call, and they're going to take heat for it in the short term. Power users are already complaining about pricing. The "Claude is getting worse" narrative conveniently overlaps with people being annoyed about paying more.

But the alternative - pretending that unlimited AI is free and sustainable - is worse. OpenAI already admitted the math doesn't work. Google is burning through compute at a rate that makes their CFO uncomfortable. The only question was who would be the first to correct pricing. Anthropic went first.

The irony is that this probably makes Anthropic the best-positioned company if there's a market correction in AI. When the music stops and investors start asking "but how much of that usage is actually profitable?", Anthropic is the one company that can say: all of it. Every token we served, someone paid for.

That's not exciting. It's not hype-friendly. But it's how you build a real business.
