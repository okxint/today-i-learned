# TSMC just posted $35.6 billion in one quarter. The company nobody talks about is the most important company in AI.

**Date:** 2026-04-12
**Topic:** AI / Semiconductors

---

Everyone's arguing about OpenAI vs Anthropic vs Google. Meanwhile the company that actually makes all their chips just posted the best quarter in semiconductor history and nobody outside finance twitter even noticed.

TSMC - Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company - reported Q1 2026 revenue of $35.6 billion. That's a 35% jump year-on-year. A new all-time record. March alone was up 45.2% from last year. And every dollar of that outperformance came from one thing: AI chip demand.

This isn't a bubble story. This is the plumbing.

## The numbers that matter

TSMC fabricates roughly nine out of every ten advanced AI accelerators on the planet. When Nvidia needs its H100s and B200s built, TSMC. When Apple needs its M-series silicon, TSMC. When AMD, Google, Amazon, and Microsoft need custom chips, TSMC. There is no plan B for most of these companies.

Here's what TSMC's customer list looks like in practice:

- **Nvidia** - about to become TSMC's largest customer, overtaking Apple for the first time ever
- **Apple** - currently the biggest customer but losing the top spot to Nvidia this year
- **AMD** - all data center GPUs manufactured at TSMC
- **Google** - TPU chips for AI training
- **Amazon** - Trainium and Graviton chips (Amazon's chip business just hit $20B annual run rate)

TSMC controls roughly 70% of the global advanced chip foundry market. Their 3nm and 5nm process technologies - the ones critical for energy-efficient AI accelerators - are accounting for a growing share of revenue with premium pricing on top.

They also hiked prices on their most advanced nodes. Analysts say that's a "big factor" behind the Q1 beat. Translation: demand is so strong they can charge more and customers still can't get enough capacity.

## The supply problem nobody can solve

Here's where it gets interesting. TSMC is telling its biggest customers it literally cannot meet demand. The bottleneck isn't the chips themselves - it's the packaging.

Advanced AI chips need something called CoWoS - Chip on Wafer on Substrate - which is a way of stacking multiple chips together to maximize performance. Almost all of this happens in Asia. Nvidia has reserved the majority of TSMC's CoWoS capacity, and it's so overbooked that TSMC has started outsourcing some steps to third-party companies.

CoWoS capacity is growing at 80% compound annual growth rate. That sounds fast until you realize AI chip demand is growing faster. TSMC is spending $52-56 billion in capex next year (up from $40.9B in 2025) and building new advanced packaging facilities in Arizona. It's still not going to be enough.

This is the chokepoint of the entire AI industry. Every model, every chatbot, every AI agent, every autonomous vehicle - all of it runs on chips that go through TSMC's fabs. And TSMC can't build fabs fast enough.

## Nvidia vs Apple - the changing of the guard

The most significant shift buried in TSMC's numbers: Nvidia is about to overtake Apple as TSMC's single largest customer. Jensen Huang confirmed it himself.

Think about what that means. For over a decade, Apple was the anchor tenant of the world's most important chip factory. The iPhone drove TSMC's revenue, expansion plans, and R&D priorities. Now it's AI training clusters.

Apple is reportedly looking for alternative chip partners because AI demand is eating into their capacity. Apple - the company with more cash than most countries - is getting squeezed out of the chip supply chain by AI demand. That's how intense this market is.

## Intel's second chance

While TSMC is supply-constrained, Intel is sitting on foundry capacity nobody wants. But that might be changing.

Intel's shares are up 19% this year. Trump praised their latest processors publicly. And analysts are starting to see Intel as a "pressure valve" for the overheated supply chain. If TSMC can't build enough chips, maybe Intel's fabs in the US and Europe can absorb some overflow.

It's still early. Intel's process technology is behind TSMC's. But the gap is narrowing, and when you're the only alternative to a company that can't meet demand, being second best is actually a pretty good business.

## The $690B connection

This is the other side of the story I wrote about two weeks ago. The hyperscalers are spending $690 billion on AI infrastructure in 2026. That money has to go somewhere. A massive chunk goes to Nvidia for GPUs. And Nvidia sends that to TSMC to actually build them.

TSMC's revenue growth is the single best proof that the AI infrastructure buildout is real, not just PowerPoint decks and press releases. You can fake a product announcement. You can inflate user metrics. You cannot fake $35.6 billion in semiconductor revenue.

The AI industry has two layers. The top layer is the one everyone sees - ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, the demos, the hype. The bottom layer is TSMC, running 24/7, unable to keep up with demand, quietly becoming the most strategically important company on earth.

TSMC expects AI chips to grow at a "mid-to-high-fifties" compound growth rate through 2029. If they're right, their AI chip revenue alone will be larger than most tech companies' total revenue within three years.

## My take

The AI bubble debate misses the point when you look at TSMC's numbers. This isn't pets.com. The demand is physically real - it shows up as silicon wafers, not slide decks.

But TSMC's dominance is also the biggest single point of failure in the entire AI industry. 70% market share in advanced foundry. 90% of AI accelerators. One island in the Taiwan Strait. The geopolitical risk isn't hypothetical - it's the reason the US is spending billions to bring chip manufacturing onshore, and it's the reason China is investing heavily in domestic alternatives.

The most important company in AI isn't OpenAI ($25B revenue) or Nvidia ($130B revenue). It's TSMC ($142B revenue, if Q1 pace holds). They don't build the models. They don't train the AI. They build the thing that everything else runs on.

And right now, they can't build it fast enough. That tells you more about the state of AI than any benchmark or product launch ever could.
