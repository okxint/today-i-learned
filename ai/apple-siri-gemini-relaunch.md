# Apple Outsourced Siri's Brain to Google — And That's the Smart Move

**March 10, 2026**

Apple is paying Google ~$1B/year to power the next Siri with a custom 1.2T-parameter Gemini model. The deal, announced January 12, drops with iOS 26.4 this month. OpenAI reportedly passed on the deeper integration — so Google won by default.

The new Siri gets on-screen awareness (sees what's on your screen and acts on it), multi-step action chaining, and real conversations instead of one-shot commands. But here's what makes it interesting architecturally: Gemini runs on Apple's Private Cloud Compute servers, not Google's. Apple is essentially renting Google's brain but locking it in Apple's house, under Apple's privacy rules. Zero data goes to Google. No Google branding visible to users.

Wall Street loved it — Alphabet briefly touched $4T valuation on announcement day. Dan Ives at Wedbush called it "an incremental positive" but noted it reveals Apple "is still struggling to build its own LLM." Fortune framed it as "Google wins, OpenAI loses."

Some features may already be delayed. 9to5Mac reports Apple is pushing certain Gemini-powered capabilities to iOS 26.5 or even iOS 27. And EU regulators are watching — the deal creates fresh antitrust exposure for Google given its existing search dominance.

**My take:** This is the most important AI partnership of 2026 so far. Apple, the king of vertical integration, just admitted it can't build a competitive LLM in-house. But the privacy wrapper is genuinely clever — it lets Apple maintain its brand promise while shipping a competitive product. The real question: does this become a permanent dependency, or is Apple building its own models behind the scenes? If Siri is still running on Gemini in 2028, Apple has a strategic problem.
